Current Lead Project

UNIGRID sodium-ion BESS integration for utility-scale validation

This current project focuses on the integration, assembly, and validation of two 150 kWh cabinet-style sodium-ion battery energy storage systems at Khalifa University for field-ready validation with ACWA Power.

I am leading the power electronics, grid integration, and control-system work, including converter selection, electrical architecture, communications, protection design, and system-level testing.

Platform: two 150 kWh Na-ion BESS cabinets Inverter target: minimum 100 kW bidirectional inverter per unit AC side: 400 Vac three-phase plus neutral Functions: grid-forming capability and ancillary services
  • Project scope includes detailed engineering review, BOM finalization, electrical integration, controller and HMI development, safety integration, and final prototype testing.
  • The system is being designed around bidirectional grid-tied operation, outdoor-rated cabinets, thermal management, and utility-facing safety requirements.
  • The testing path includes staged power-up, low-power verification, charge-discharge cycles, and full-power grid-connected validation.
  • The project combines battery integration, power conversion, communication design, automation logic, and field-ready documentation.

Industrial R&D Case Study

10 kW transformerless DVC development at OKY

At OKY, I worked on a three-phase 10 kW transformerless dynamic voltage compensator for 220 V operation, combining a high-current bidirectional DC-DC converter with a three-phase inverter stage. The system was designed around supercapacitor energy storage and fast mode transition requirements for voltage sag support.

I carried out the complete hardware design and controller design for the platform.

Input side: 25-50 V supercapacitor interface up to 400 A DC link: 400 V Output: 10 kW three-phase inverter / DVC platform Target transfer: less than 0.5 ms
  • Used a two-stage architecture: interleaved boost on the low-voltage side and dual active bridge on the high-voltage side.
  • Handled complete hardware design, including module design, PCB/layout decisions, sensing integration, and control-board interfacing.
  • Designed the controller structure for the DC-DC and inverter sections, including mode handling, voltage regulation, and system coordination.
  • Designed around a difficult engineering regime where 400 A input current created major thermal, layout, and efficiency challenges.
  • Integrated bidirectional operation so the supercapacitor side could both discharge to support the inverter and be charged in reverse power flow.

MSc Project

5 kW inverter design during MSc at Soongsil University

During my MSc at Soongsil University, I designed and tested a 5 kW bidirectional inverter system for AC/DC microgrid applications in the RECL laboratory.

  • Designed the inverter for both inverter and rectifier mode operation with bidirectional power flow.
  • Implemented ripple-correlation MPPT and average DC-link voltage control.
  • Developed current-control strategies in rotating and stationary reference frames.
  • Used the platform for harmonic compensation and islanding detection verification during my master's work.
