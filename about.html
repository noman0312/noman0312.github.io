About & Contact

Power electronics researcher focused on practical converter control and resilient renewable-energy systems.

I work across power electronics, power systems, grid-connected and grid-forming converters, nonlinear control, and stability in renewable-rich grids, with academic and industrial experience across Pakistan, South Korea, and the United Arab Emirates.

My work combines control design, system-level stability analysis, embedded implementation, and laboratory validation for converter-dominated energy systems.

Research Outcomes

Representative results

  • Developed a virtual-oscillator-based PLL for robust synchronization under weak and distorted grid conditions.
  • Proposed a harmonic compensation method that achieved more than 80% reduction in current THD, with less than 2.5% THD under adverse operating conditions.
  • Designed harmonic compensation for a 5 kW bidirectional inverter achieving less than 5% THD across the full load range.
  • Proposed an energy-based damping framework for mitigating inter-converter oscillations in grid-forming systems.
  • Established a research direction connecting converter-level control design with system-level stability in future renewable-heavy power grids.
