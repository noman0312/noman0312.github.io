Technical Capabilities

Experimental & Software Skills

A breakdown of my hands-on experience spanning high-power converter prototyping, digital control implementation, and power systems simulation.

Hardware & Laboratory

Experimental Skills

  • Converter Hardware Design: Comprehensive design, prototyping, and testing of high-power converter platforms (up to 10 kW).
  • PCB Design: Multi-layer PCB routing for gate drivers, sensing chains, and DSP interfaces.
  • DSP Firmware Implementation: Hands-on programming of Texas Instruments (TI) C2000 DSPs for complex control algorithms.
  • dSPACE Systems: Rapid control prototyping using dSPACE controllers for grid-interaction studies.
  • OPAL-RT HIL Simulation: Real-time hardware-in-the-loop (HIL) modeling and validation, backed by a comprehensive 4-day experimental training certification from OPAL-RT.
  • Measurement & Validation: Proficiency with advanced oscilloscopes, power analyzers, and programmable AC/DC sources/loads for rigorous experimental validation.
  • System Integration: Building complete hardware setups including sensing circuits, LCL filters, and testing validation.
Simulation & Programming

Software Skills

  • MATLAB/Simulink: Deep expertise in modeling power systems, nonlinear control, grid-forming strategies, and automated code generation.
  • C / C++: Embedding low-level firmware for microcontrollers and high-speed closed-loop controls.
  • PSCAD: Electromagnetic transient simulation for power systems and converter-dominated grids.
  • Code Composer Studio (CCS): TI's IDE for compiling and debugging embedded DSP systems.
  • Altium Designer / KiCad: Schematic capture and integrated PCB layout.
  • Data Analysis & Plotting: Processing experimental data for academic publication and comprehensive evaluation.
Implementation Reality

Bridging the gap between theory and hardware.

My research philosophy heavily emphasizes experimental validation. I design the hardware, write the firmware, build the simulation models, and validate everything on the test bench to ensure control concepts work in the physical world under real grid conditions.
